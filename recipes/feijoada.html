Black Bean Feijoada

from "The Intolerant Gourmet" by Barbara Kafka, pg. 150

Description

This is the only recipe on the site that I have not made yet, but I'm looking forward to trying it. I have stared dabbling with the Tim Ferriss "Slow-Carb" Diet, which looks a lot like my standard Protein-Veg-Starch diet, but replaces your standard starches with beans and lentils. I went hunting for some fun bean-based recipes to try out and found this one.

This dish is a hack of a hack. Feijoada is a Brazilian dish that is typically made with pork. The recipe in "The Intolerant Gourmet" is a vegan version served over rice. My take is to skip the rice and just have the beans and sauce as a fun protein-packed side."

Ingredients

Beans

  • 2 cups dried black beans
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, smashed, peeled, and minced
  • 1 tablespoon saffloweer oil
  • 6 cups vegetable broth or water
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1/4 tsp freshly gound black pepper
  • 2 oranges, halved
  • Kosher salt
  • 2 stalks celery, peeled and chopped*

    • *I for one have never heard of peeling celery before.

Sauce

  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 2 tomatoes, peeled
  • 1 small onion, quartered
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1 tsp chili sauce
  • 1 fresh hot green chili, stemmed (optional)
  • 1/4 cup wine or cider vinegar
  • Kosher salt

Steps

  1. Soak the beans in stock or water overnight.
  2. In a Dutch oven, saute the onion and garlic in the oil until the onion is tender.
  3. Add the drained beans, broth, bay leaf, and pepper.
  4. Bring the beans to a boil, add the oranges, salt to taste, and the celery.
  5. Cover the Dutch oven and simmer for 1 hour, then remove the lid and simmer for another 1-2 hours.
  6. When the beans are tender, remove about one-third of them and mash.
  7. Return the beans to the pot and continue cooking until the mashed beans thicken the mixture.
  8. When you're ready to serve, put all the sauce ingredients, including salt to taste, in a blender and liquify
  9. Stir sauce into the feijoada and serve.
