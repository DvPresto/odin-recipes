Instant Pot Tuscan Chicken Stew

from Real Food Whole Life

Description

It's finally stew season, so it's time to get the Instant Pot in full rotation. Full Disclosure: I am an Instant Pot evangelist, and I think Robyn's recipes on realfoodwholelife.com are some of the easiest and tastiest ways to make great use out of the gadget.

This chicken stew is a straight-forward recipe perfect for dinner on those nights when you start to feel a chill in the air. It's also a nice way to get an Italian flavor profile into a gluten-free meal plan.

Ingredients

  • 1lb boneless skinless chicken thighs
  • 1 cup chopped carrots
  • 1 cup chopped celery
  • 1 cup chopped onion
  • 1 cup chopped yellow potato
  • 1 15-oz can diced fire-roasted tomatoes
  • 1 6-oz can tomato paste
  • 1 tsp Italian seasoning
  • 1 tsp granulated garlic
  • 32-oz chicken or vegetable stock
  • 1 tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt
  • 2 tbsp arrowroot powder or cornstarch

Steps

  1. Set the Instant Pot to saute and heat 1 tbsp of olive oil.
  2. Add carrots, celery, and onion and cook for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Add chicken and cook for 3 minutes on each side, until lightly browned.
  4. Add tomato paste, Italian seasoning, and granulated garlic then cook for 1 minute, stirring occasionally.
  5. Turn off saute and add the potato, tomatoes, stock, salt, and 1 tbsp each of olive oil and balsamic vinegar.
  6. Stir to combine, then cook on high pressure for 6 minutes (natural release).
  7. Remove chicken, shred or dice, then add back to the pot.
  8. Mix arrowroot powder with 1/4 cup water, the pour misture into the pot, stirring to combine.
  9. Serve with a drizzle of olive oil and fresh ground pepper to taste.
